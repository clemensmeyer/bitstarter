Access to the Internet is the basis for information, education and empowerment in the modern world. Still, almost 800 million people living in rural India do not have access to the Internet, as they are lacking an Internet connection, a computer, or even electricity.

cycy brings Internet to rural India - one rickshaw at a time. Each rickshaw is equipped with a 3G WiFi router to provide a local Internet connection, and several tablet computers to access the Internet. Power is provided by a battery that is charged over night as well as a bike dynamo for emergencies.

In order to provide Internet access to as many people as possible, each rickshaw serves several villages, travelling from one spot to the other on a fixed schedule.

We need your support to make this project come true. Provide funding for us to build the first ten cycys and a base station, and run them for a full year! This is your unique opportunity to empower thousands of people.

FAQ

What does cycy stand for?
Cycy stands for cyber cycle, which describes the use of a rickshaw to provide Internet access.
Why should I support you?
We consider access to the Internet a quasi human right, as it enables information and education, and can thus help to create a better, more fair world. Cycy is a highly cost-efficient way to bring affordable Internet access to the rural Indian population.
What are the origins of this project?
The basic idea of cycy was developed in the class Strategy & Sustainable Development at HEC Paris.
What will my money be used for?
Your money will be used to manufacture the first 15 cycy bikes as well as to operate and maintain them over the first year. After the first year, the business will be sustainable, and we will reinvest any profits into expanding our offering to more villages and people.
